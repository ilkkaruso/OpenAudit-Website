OpenAudit Philippines

Making Governance Data Accessible

About This Project

OpenAudit Philippines is part of a broader initiative that uses natural language processing and machine learning to transform public audit reports into structured, research-ready datasets. The project aims to make governance data more accessible to academics, researchers, journalists, and accountability advocates.

Publicly-available audit reports from government institutions represent an underutilized resource. These documents contain detailed financial and operational data about government decisions, yet remain largely inaccessible to researchers and the public due to their format and volume.

This visualization focuses on Commission on Audit (COA) reports from the Philippines spanning 2016-2022, extracting insights about transparency, accountability, and compliance with audit recommendations—metrics not captured by standard governance indicators like the Corruption Perception Index or World Governance Indicators.

Commission on Audit (COA) Annual Audit Reports

The Commission on Audit is the supreme audit institution of the Philippines, mandated by the 1987 Constitution to examine and audit all government agencies, including local government units. Each year, COA releases Annual Audit Reports (AARs) for every municipality containing:

  • Audit observations - Issues identified during the audit
  • Recommendations - Specific actions the municipality should take
  • Management actions - The municipality's response or actions taken
  • Status of prior year's recommendations - Whether previous recommendations were implemented

Data Extraction

Audit observations were extracted from COA AARs using a combination of PDF parsing and natural language processing. The implementation status of each recommendation was classified as:

Status Description
IMPLEMENTED Recommendation was fully addressed by the municipality
PARTIALLY IMPLEMENTED Some action taken but recommendation not fully addressed
NOT IMPLEMENTED No meaningful action taken on the recommendation
UNKNOWN Status could not be determined from available data

Note: Observations with UNKNOWN status are excluded from compliance calculations. Only observations with a determinable implementation status contribute to a municipality's compliance score.

Compliance Scoring

Each municipality receives a Compliance Score based on the percentage of audit recommendations that were implemented. The score reflects the rate of non-implementation, where:

  • Lower scores (0-20) = Very High Compliance - most recommendations implemented
  • Higher scores (80-100) = Very Low Compliance - most recommendations not implemented
Score = (Not Implemented + 0.5 × Partially Implemented) / Total Known × 100

Partially implemented recommendations are weighted at 50% to reflect partial progress toward compliance.

Limitations & Caveats

  • Data extraction accuracy: Automated extraction from PDF documents may introduce errors. Some status classifications may be incorrect.
  • Unknown status observations: Observations with unknown status are excluded from calculations. Their exclusion may affect results if the unknown status is systematically related to non-compliance.
  • Not a direct measure of corruption: Non-implementation of audit recommendations does not necessarily indicate corruption. It may reflect capacity constraints, resource limitations, or disagreement with recommendations.
  • Coverage gaps: Some municipalities may be missing or have incomplete data across all years due to PDF extraction failures or PSGC matching issues.
  • Equal weighting: All recommendations are weighted equally. A minor procedural recommendation and a major financial control recommendation contribute the same to the score.
